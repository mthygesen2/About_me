My life in a nutshell

Hello again! Thanks for wanting to read a little more about me. I was born in Japan and raised there until the young age of 5 and a half. We got up and moved to beautiful Seattle, Washington where I went to school and eventually graduated from University of Washington. I went to school for International Studies and after graduating I got up and moved my life over to Yokosuka, Japan. And since then I have been bitten by the travel bug.

I taught English at an elementary school in Japan for 3 year and aboslutely loved every minute of it. I suddenly decided to uproot my life again and move to Melbourne, Australia. I got a working holiday visa (which I suggest everyone under the age of 30 to go ahead and do) and worked while checking out all the great things Australia has to offer.

I ended up back in the Pacific Northwest in lovely Portland, Oregon. Close enough to home but far enough where the tranisition back to American living wasn't such a shock. I worked as a bartender at Bamboo Sushi and eventually got myself signed up at Epicodus where I am now.

What makes me excited about life

  • Traveling. If I could, I would never settle and be a nomad for life.
  • Food. I enjoy everything about it. Seeing where it comes from, catching it, growing it, cooking/baking it and of course eating it.
  • Scuba diving. There is something about seeing the other 70% of the world and singing "Under the Sea" while diving.
  • Cats. I swear I am not a crazy cat lady but I think they are awesome. My cats Uni and Ebi are pretty little things. But also my parents dog, Blue.
